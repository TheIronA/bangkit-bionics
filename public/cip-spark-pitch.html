Project Description
Project Description

The Problem

  • Upper-limb amputation in Malaysia is driven by industrial accidents and road trauma — predominantly affecting B40 working-age adults.
  • SOCSO covers RM 2,000. Imported myoelectric hands that restore hand function cost RM 20,000–80,000. Government hospital budgets don't cover the gap.
  • Most B40 amputees go without a functional hand — or take on debt for a device with no local support.
  • Across Southeast Asia: 180,000 new upper-limb amputees each year. No affordable myoelectric option exists for the majority of the working-age population.

Our Solution

  • Myoelectric prosthetic hand reading EMG + force signals. On-device neural network classifies intended grip in <5ms — no phone, no cloud.
  • Motorised thumb automates grip repositioning. 3D-scanned, patient-specific socket addresses the #1 cause of prosthetic abandonment: poor fit.
  • Target price: RM 4,500–14,500 — a 5–10× reduction vs. comparable devices.

Anticipated Outcomes (18 Months)

  • Working MVP prototype — sensors, classifier, actuators, and patient-specific socket integrated into one wearable device.
  • 1-patient technical usability validation — MREC-approved clinical session with one amputee pilot, capturing grip accuracy and comfort data.
  • Regulatory foundation — QMS framework initiated, MDA pre-submission completed, MREC approval in hand.

Potential Impact

  • First locally-manufactured, clinically-validated myoelectric hand in Malaysia — building regional prosthetics manufacturing capability.
  • Directly enables NTIS Sandbox 1 (RM 250K) application, funding formal multi-patient clinical trials for commercial entry.
  • Long-term: scalable to 180,000 annual amputees across Southeast Asia.

Technology Benchmarking
Technology Benchmarking

Our Uniqueness

  • FMG+EMG sensor fusion — peer-validated at >97% accuracy (Ke et al., Sensors 2020).
  • On-device TFLite Micro on nRF52840 — <5ms inference, no connectivity dependency.
  • Motorised, auto-repositioning thumb — absent from all devices in this price tier.
  • Patient-specific 3D-printed socket — scanned and printed per patient, not off-the-shelf.
  • Local manufacturing — Malaysia base, Singapore clinical entry; prosthetist-serviced.

Competitive Threats & Barriers

  • Ottobock & Össur — dominant globally, but priced SGD 15K–50K+. Serve premium private market only.
  • Open Bionics (Hero Arm) — UK-based, CE-marked, priced ~SGD 13K–17K. Motorized flexion but requires manual rotation for different grips; no SEA clinical presence.
  • Vulcan Augmetics — SEA-based, ~USD 1,100. Uses ML-based control but depends on manual thumb rotation for grip switching—the #1 user complaint.
  • Barriers to new entrants: regulatory (MREC, MDA, CE Mark), clinical partnerships, and local manufacturing know-how — all areas we are actively building.

Substitutes & Our Advantage

  • Body-powered hooks — RM 500–2,000, no grip automation, high abandonment. Our device outperforms on function.
  • Cosmetic prosthetics — no functional value; only address appearance.
  • Our moat: affordable price + motorised thumb + on-device AI + local clinical support + patient-specific fit. No single competitor offers all five.
